  • 2 and 9 March 2016: Data hack on footfall counts in Leeds: data driven (agent based) models (Jon Ward) in Physics Research Deck SR 9.301 (Looks like our old mall)
    This session will start with an open discussion about data driven modelling and how to incorporate statistical analyses of data into agent based models. We'll then discuss a specific problem: how to model the movement of people through the centre of Leeds using footfall data. We will do this in the style of a `data hack' using openly available footfall data from the Leeds Data Mill:http://leedsdatamill.org/dataset/leeds-city-centre-footfall-data. As a collective, we will determine what questions need to be asked of the data and how we might incorporate the answers into a predictive model. I would like this session to be highly interactive and for the data hack I strongly encourage you to bring a laptop that runs any data analysis software that you would normally use. However, I am happy for those who don't want to bring a laptop to be my back seat drivers (although I'll be doing this in python/matlab, not R ;).
